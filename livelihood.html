Livelihood

Livelihood for every family in a community means increased income that translates to food, education, health care, and asset creation. Afosi works with enterprising young people and women to build competitive enterprises by addressing problems of capacity, production, access, and income. Our programs are designed to bolster agricultural production, jumpstart local market activity, support micro-enterprise initiatives, and enhance a vulnerable communityâ€™s access to sustainable sources of food and income. Our livelihood program targets youth and women in secondary schools

Our outcomes are:

  • Improved capacity of small holder farmers and entrepreneurs
  • Increased access to markets
  • Increased incomes for the youth and women
  • Increased access to financing

CAPACITY DEVELOPMENT

Many smallholder farmers and entrepreneurs lack links to local markets and there is huge potential to push up sales. Afosi works to support farmers to build groups and establish collection centers so they can sell in bulk. We also help businesses gain access to the finance they need and provide training on how to professionalise and fulfill their potential.

Our Intervention
  1. Village Savings and Loans Group
    • Builds the economic resilience and financial literacy of households through the mobilization of self-help mechanisms on savings and credit services.
  2. Local Value Chain Development
    • links farmers and producers to markets by closely collaborating with market players supporting enterprises and industries.

ENTERPRISE DEVELOPMENT AND JOB CREATION

Increasing employment opportunities for youth, and strengthening the ecosystem, so that youth and women can find good jobs and contribute to the economic growth of the country..

Our Intervention
  • Digital literacy
  • Work readiness skills
  • 21st century skills training
  • Capacity Development: skilling, upskilling, re-skilling, match making, linkages, apprenticeships
