Health

Health is an asset that poor people would not want to lose. Indeed, their livelihoods depend on their health. When a poor person becomes sick, is injured or is unwell, the entire household and even entire communities get affected. Young people and women are the most affected by poor health. Therefore, for sustainable development to be attained, communities and governments must invest in the sexual reproductive health and rights of their young people especially women who form the future of a population.

  • Quality of health data for effective programming

Sexual Reproductive Health and Rights

Sustainable Development Goals have prioritised health through SDG 3, ensure healthy lives and promote wellbeing for all at all ages. The goal urges governments and communities to ensure universal access to sexual and reproductive health-care services, including for family planning, information and education, and the integration of reproductive health into national strategies and programmes by 2030

  • Comprehensive SRHR Education
  • Community Health Strategy
  • Advocacy and Lobbying initiatives
  • Innovations for health

At Afosi, we work with communities, young people and women, county & national governments to promote access and utilization of sexual and reproductive health and rights services amongst the rightsholders. We utilize evidence-based interventions to reach to the populations that have been for long left behind. We use data to inform our programming and advocacy agenda so as to achieve optimal impact from the resources we have.
